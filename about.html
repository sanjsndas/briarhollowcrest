About Briar · forest & fungi

Briar Hollowcrest Moss is a living journal, a mycelial archive, and a quiet gathering place for those who listen to the forest. We are rooted in the old ways, yet reaching toward new understandings of ecology, art, and the wisdom of mushrooms.

🌲 · under the canopy

Our roots

Founded in 2020, Briar Hollowcrest Moss began as a personal field journal — sketches of fungi, observations of mycorrhizal networks, and recipes for forest dyes. Over time, it grew into a collaborative space where mycologists, foragers, artists, and forest walkers share their discoveries.

We believe that fungi are the unseen architects of the forest, weaving together life in ways we are only beginning to understand. Our work is dedicated to deepening that understanding through storytelling, illustration, and hands-on practice.

What we carry

🌿 Ecological wisdom

We study the forest as a living network, learning from indigenous knowledge, field science, and direct observation. Every essay is rooted in respect for the land.

🍄 Craft & art

From spore prints to fungal dyes, we celebrate the beauty of fungi through traditional craft and contemporary art. Our guides are illustrated with care.

📜 Slow knowledge

We resist the rush of modern information. Here, knowledge ripens slowly — through seasons, through patience, through the quiet work of roots and hyphae.

🌱 6+ seasons of field observations 📖 200+ pages of mycelial notes

The keeper

— Briar Hollowcrest

Briar is a mycological artist, forest guide, and writer. With a background in ethnobotany and a deep connection to the Caledonian woods, she has spent the last decade documenting fungal life and teaching others to see the forest floor with new eyes.

Her work has been featured in Mycologia, Forest Ecology and Art, and she is the author of The Hyphae Chronicles (2023).
